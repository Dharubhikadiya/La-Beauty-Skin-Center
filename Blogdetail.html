Does Tattoo Removal Work On All Skin Types?

Yes, tattoo removal generally works on all skin types. However, tattoo removal on dark skin has specific considerations. The speed at which results become apparent depends mainly on the amount of melanin in the skin.

Those with lighter skin may see quicker results in fewer sessions compared to those who go in for dark skin tattoo removal.

This is because the laser that removes ink from the skin is attracted to darker pigments like tattoo ink. As such, lighter skin with less melanin allows the laser to accurately focus on darker pigments. However, laser tattoo removal on dark skin may be a challenge, as the mechanism may have difficulty differentiating tattoo ink from darker skin pigments.

Impact of Skin Tone on Duration of Laser Tattoo Removal

There are six types of skin tones per the Fitzpatrick Scale. Type 1 is the lightest, which is very sensitive to sunlight, gets easily sunburnt, and doesn’t tan. On the other end of the scale, Type 6 is the darkest skin tone. Those with this type don’t get sunburnt but tan relatively easily.

The Fitzpatrick scale demonstrates how different skin tones react to UV radiation from sunlight. Melanin absorbs heat, so based on this scale, laser technicians must determine the proper wavelength that will prevent injuries and adverse reactions.

In general, dark skin tattoo removal and higher melanin concentration tend to take longer than tattoo removal on brown skin and significantly lighter skin types.

Fitzpatrick Skin Types

Pale white skin: very light, extremely sensitive skin that burns but doesn’t tan

White skin: very sensitive skin that burns quickly and tans minimally

Light rown skin: sensitive skin that sometimes burns and slowly tans to light brown

Light brown skin: sensitive skin that sometimes burns and slowly tans to light brown

Dark brown skin: very robust skin that rarely burns and tans well

Deeply pigmented dark brown to black tone skin: very resistant skin tone

What Are Possible Side Effects of Laser Tattoo Removal for Darker Skin Tones?

Tattoo removal on dark skin is generally safe, but some of the rare side effects are hyperpigmentation, or the darkening of skin over time; hypopigmentation, the lightening of the skin in the area where the tattoo was removed; and keloid scars, which are raised scar tissues that develop around the treated area 

Risk of Tattoo Removal for Dark Skin

Laser tattoo removal on dark skin is generally safe, especially when done using the right wavelength. As long as the laser technician consults the Fitzpatrick scale to determine the amount of melanin and the effective wavelength needed, they should know the safest method for your treatment. 

Burning is a serious risk among darker skin types. Much like wearing a dark shirt on a hot day, melanin’s capacity to absorb heat can lead to the skin taking in too much energy from the laser in dark skin tattoo removal. This can result in skin discoloration and discomfort, plus long-term side effects. 

Laser tattoo removal on dark skin requires a careful, much more considered approach than for clients with lighter skin.
